MeloPost User Guide

Comprehensive manual for incident postmortems and systematic root cause analysis.

1 Introduction

MeloPost is a specialized application designed to help organizations move beyond simple "blame games" and into structured, systematic root cause analysis. By using the Swiss Cheese Model, MeloPost helps teams identify not just who made a mistake, but what layers of defense failed and how to prevent similar issues in the future.

Figure 1: The MeloPost Dashboard provides a central hub for incident management.

2 Key Concepts

The Swiss Cheese Model

Defenses are likened to slices of Swiss cheese. Slices (Layers) represent defenses. Holes represent weaknesses. Incidents occur when holes in every slice align.

Layers, Holes & Stories

  • Layer: Category of defense (e.g., Run, Test).
  • Hole: A specific weakness found in a layer.
  • Story: A remedial task created to plug a hole.

3 Getting Started

Upon logging in, you are greeted with the Dashboard. Here you can see key metrics and recent postmortems. Use the search bar to find specific incidents using keywords or tags.

Figure 2: Dashboard Overview and Search.

4 Managing Postmortems

Click "Create New Postmortem" to begin. You'll need to provide basic incident data, including title, date, type (Local, Major, Orchestrated P1), and references to external systems.

Figure 3: Incident creation form.

5 In-Depth Incident Analysis

Timeline & Details

The details view is where you manage the timeline, analyze layers, and document holes. Every postmortem starts with a set of 10 standard defense layers.

Figure 4 & 5: Postmortem details view and Timeline management.

Cheese Layers & Holes

Analyze each layer to identify where the defense failed. Add "Holes" to document these specific failures and assign remedial actions.

Figure 6 & 7: Analyzing layers and identifying holes.

Guiding Questions

Use the Questions tab to help stimulate deeper analysis and document your thought process.

Figure 8: Structured analysis through guiding questions.

6 Remedial Actions (Stories)

A postmortem is only successful if it leads to improvement. "Stories" are the mechanism for this. Link holes to specific tasks in platforms like Jira, ServiceNow, or Azure DevOps.

Figure 9: Remedial stories management.
